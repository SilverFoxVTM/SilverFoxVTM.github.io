Service

July 20, 2026
In Virtual Truck Manager 3, the primary difference is speed, cost, and location. On-base services require returning your truck to your own depot for cheaper, scheduled maintenance, while emergency services (roadside assistance) provide immediate repairs anywhere on the map, but at a heavily inflated cost. [1, 2]
Breakdown of the Differences
FeatureEmergency ServicesOn Base Services
LocationAnywhere on the map (wherever the truck broke down or stalled).Strictly at your company's own Depot/Base.
CostVery high (premium/penalty rates for on-the-spot roadside rescue).Standard/Lower cost (uses your in-house mechanics or base facilities).
Wait Time/LogisticsRequires time for emergency crews to arrive or tow the vehicle.Requires your truck to drive back to the base, taking it out of active transit.
Driver StatusMay resolve driver sickness/fatigue on the spot or recover a stranded load.Tied to your terminal; requires managing rest schedules or hiring replacement staff.
Best Practices
  • Use On-Base Services for regular preventive maintenance to keep your long-term operating costs down.
  • Keep Emergency Services as a backup safety net for when a vehicle breaks down in the middle of a high-value, time-sensitive contract to avoid heavy delivery penalties

 

In Virtual Truck Manager 3, the exact cost of an emergency service (roadside assistance or towing) varies dynamically. It depends on factors like distance from a depot, the severity of the breakdown, and your truck's current condition. [1, 2]
Because emergencies carry high base fees and eat heavily into your profit margins, the best practice is to proactively prevent them: [1, 2]
  • Hire Technicians: Ensure you have mechanics on your roster at your hubs. Your trucks will undergo routine maintenance automatically to prevent breakdowns if you have the funds.
  • Manage Wear & Tear: Keep a close eye on your dashboard KPIs to spot failing parts before the truck gets stranded on a route.
  • Upgrade Depots: Expanding your warehouses and offices helps streamline realistic repair cycles across Europe and North America.
